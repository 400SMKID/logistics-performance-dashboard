# logistics-performance-dashboard
Logistics Performance Dashboard
Project Overview

This project was developed to analyze logistics operations and identify the main factors contributing to shipment delays. Using Power BI, Power Query, and DAX, an interactive dashboard was created to evaluate asset utilization, waiting times, inventory levels, shipment status, and overall operational performance.

The objective of the analysis was to transform operational data into actionable insights that support better decision-making and improve logistics efficiency.

Business Problem

The organization was experiencing a high percentage of delayed shipments and needed to understand the operational factors driving these delays.

Key questions addressed:

How many shipments are being processed?
What percentage of shipments are delayed?
Which assets are the most utilized?
Which assets generate the highest waiting times?
What factors contribute to shipment delays?
Which assets perform most efficiently?
Dataset

The dataset contains:

1,000 shipment records
Asset utilization information
Waiting time metrics
Logistics delay indicators
Inventory levels
Shipment status data
Geographic coordinates

Dashboard Structure
Page 1 – Executive Logistics Dashboard

Provides a high-level overview of operational performance through KPIs and summary visualizations.

Key Metrics:

Total Shipments
Delay Rate
Average Waiting Time
Average Inventory Level
Asset Utilization
Active Assets

Main Findings:

1,000 shipments analyzed.
57% of shipments experienced delays.
Operational activity is concentrated in a small number of vessels.
Several assets show significantly higher waiting times than the fleet average.
Page 2 – Delay Root Cause Analysis

Focuses on identifying the operational factors behind shipment delays.

Analysis Areas:

Delay Rate by Asset
Asset Utilization Ranking
Waiting Time Distribution
Waiting Time vs Asset Utilization
Operational Bottlenecks

Main Findings:

Higher asset utilization is associated with longer waiting times.
Waiting times contribute directly to shipment delays.
Operational workload is concentrated on a limited number of vessels.
Resource saturation is one of the primary drivers of delays.
Page 3 – Asset Performance Analysis

Evaluates individual asset performance and compares efficiency across the fleet.

Analysis Areas:

Asset Utilization Ranking
Delay Ranking
Waiting Time Ranking
Asset Performance Matrix
Asset Efficiency Analysis

Main Findings:

Vessels 3, 2, 8, 1, and 7 account for most operational activity.
Vessels 1, 3, 7, and 6 experience the highest waiting times.
Vessel 2 was identified as the most efficient asset.
Workload balancing opportunities exist across the fleet.
Key Insights

The analysis revealed a strong relationship between asset utilization, waiting times, and shipment delays.

As utilization increases, loading activities become concentrated on specific assets, generating longer waiting times and increasing the probability of delayed shipments.

This pattern suggests that operational efficiency can be improved through better workload distribution and more balanced asset allocation strategies.

Recommendations
Redistribute workload across available assets.
Reduce dependency on heavily utilized vessels.
Optimize loading and unloading processes.
Monitor waiting time indicators continuously.
Implement performance reviews for critical assets.
Develop collaborative action plans between Logistics and Warehouse teams.
